1. A consumer group wishes to test the longevity of various brands of lightbulbs. A random sample of \(6\) bulbs from each of the four given brands’ production were chosen and lightbulbs were burnt until they failed. here are the results (numbers are hours that the bulb lasted). Data can be found in the file ob_prob_bulbs.txt.

  1. [4 points] We wish to test whether the mean longevity differs significantly from brand to brand. Write the appropriate ANOVA model: include the equation, ranges of subscripts, restriction of the parameters and distributional assumptions.

    Solution
    Model: \[ Y_{ij} = \mu + \alpha_i + \varepsilon_{ij}, \ \ \ i=1,2,3,4 \ \text{and} \ j=1,2, \dots, 6 \] where \(\varepsilon \stackrel{iid}{\sim} N(0, \sigma)\).

    “It is common, but not necessary, to impose the restriction that \(\sum_i \alpha_i = 0\).”

    “The residuals are normally distributed with equal variance.”

  2. [5 points] State the appropriate null and alternative hypotheses and perform the appropriate test. What is the P-value? Use 5% level of significance.

    Solution
    \(H_0: \mu_{X2} = \mu_{X2} = \mu_{Z1} = \mu_{Z2}\)
    \(H_a:\) at least one brand is different.
    “With the restriction succested in part(a) we can state these as \(H_0: \alpha_1=\alpha_2=\alpha_3=\alpha_4\) and \(H_a\): at least one \(\alpha_i\) is not \(0\).”

  1. [8 points] Additional information is that Brand \(X1\) and Brand \(X2\) are manufactured at Factory \(X\); Brand \(Z1\) and Brand \(Z2\) are manufactured at Factory \(Z\). With this additional information, perform the analysis according to an appropriately revised model. Write down the appropriate ANOVA model and discuss results.

    Solution
    “This is nested design. Check for factory difference, as well as two brands under each factory.” The two factors are fixed effects.
bulb.df$factory <- ifelse(test=bulb.df$brand=="X1" | bulb.df$brand=="X2", yes="X", no="Z")
bulb.df$factory <- factor(bulb.df$factory)
bulb.nest <- aov(formula=time ~ factory + factory/brand, data=bulb.df)  # nested and fixed effect
# aov(formula=time ~ factory + brand %in% factory, data=bulb.df) # equivalent nested model
